# Top 10 Reusable Patterns

These are the 10 strongest patterns worth carrying forward into how we work.

## 1. Business and engineering are different modes
Use different lenses for strategy and execution.

## 2. Files beat hidden prompts
Keep reusable behavior in markdown and docs, not buried in chat history.

## 3. Routines create leverage
Repeated work should become repeatable workflows.

## 4. Memory should be domain-shaped
Separate project memory, decisions, setup notes, and playbooks instead of dumping everything together.

## 5. Outputs should be readable artifacts
A good memo, review, dashboard, or doc is more useful than a one-off answer.

## 6. Invocation should be lightweight
Good systems are easy to call into action with plain prompts.

## 7. The assistant should help run the business, not just answer questions
Operating rhythm matters.

## 8. Swap providers, keep workflows
Tooling should not own the logic.

## 9. Specialization beats a giant blob assistant
Clear roles and lenses improve quality.

## 10. Local-first is a feature
Durability, privacy, and control matter.

## What these patterns imply for us

They push us toward:
- docs-first workflows
- lightweight skills and playbooks
- project-aware notes
- reusable operating routines
- persistent decision capture
