# Amiga Business OS

This is a lightweight business operating system for how Shaun and Amiga work together.

It borrows the best ideas from larger business-agent frameworks, but keeps them small, practical, and easy to use.

## The 10 best patterns

### 1. Daily Brief
A short daily operating view.

What it includes:
- active priorities
- urgent messages or follow-ups
- calendar pressure
- blockers
- suggested next actions

Use when:
- starting the day
- resetting after context switching
- checking whether anything important is drifting

Suggested prompt:
> Give me a daily brief for current priorities, comms, deadlines, and blockers.

---

### 2. Weekly Founder Review
A higher-level review loop.

What it includes:
- what moved this week
- what stalled
- what decisions matter now
- where energy is being wasted
- what to cut, double down on, or defer

Use when:
- planning the week
- ending the week
- feeling spread too thin

Suggested prompt:
> Run a weekly founder review across active projects and tell me what to focus, cut, and decide.

---

### 3. Opportunity Triage
Evaluate ideas fast without overcommitting.

What it includes:
- problem quality
- user pain
- speed to first proof
- defensibility
- likely distraction cost

Use when:
- a new business idea shows up
- an opportunity appears attractive but fuzzy

Suggested prompt:
> Triage this opportunity and tell me whether it deserves deeper work, a quick test, or a no.

---

### 4. Project Pulse
A concise state-of-play report for any project.

What it includes:
- current status
- recent changes
- open risks
- unknowns
- next milestones

Use when:
- restarting work
- handing context between sessions
- documenting project reality

Suggested prompt:
> Give me a project pulse for [project name].

---

### 5. Decision Log
Capture key decisions before they evaporate.

What it includes:
- decision made
- why it was made
- alternatives rejected
- implications
- what would change the decision later

Use when:
- we choose tools, architecture, workflows, or direction

Suggested prompt:
> Turn this discussion into a decision log entry.

---

### 6. Inbox to Action
Convert messages, notes, and loose ideas into action.

What it includes:
- classify input
- extract action items
- extract follow-ups
- extract references
- identify what can be ignored

Use when:
- you dump screenshots, notes, chats, or emails on me

Suggested prompt:
> Process this inbox dump into actions, references, and things to ignore.

---

### 7. Research to Memo
Turn messy research into a usable decision memo.

What it includes:
- what matters
- what is noise
- recommendations
- tradeoffs
- open questions

Use when:
- researching software, markets, tools, competitors, or plans

Suggested prompt:
> Turn this research into a decision memo with a recommendation.

---

### 8. UI De-Slop Review
A design and taste review loop for products that feel generic.

What it includes:
- where the UI feels default
- what visual system is missing
- what references fit
- what to change first
- what direction to lean into

Use when:
- the product works but feels bland
- a design system exists but has no personality

Suggested prompt:
> Review this UI and tell me exactly why it feels generic, then propose a sharper direction.

---

### 9. Routine Builder
Turn repeated tasks into reusable operating procedures.

What it includes:
- trigger
- inputs
- steps
- outputs
- review cadence

Use when:
- something keeps recurring
- the same kind of analysis keeps being requested

Suggested prompt:
> Turn this repeated workflow into a reusable routine.

---

### 10. Documentation as We Go
Write docs while the project is alive, not after memory has decayed.

What it includes:
- setup notes
- project status
- architecture notes
- operator instructions
- pitfalls and fixes

Use when:
- we solve something non-trivial
- we build something worth reusing
- we don’t want future Shaun to forget how it works

Suggested prompt:
> Document this in our project docs site.

## Recommended usage pattern

For most projects, use this rhythm:

1. Opportunity triage
2. Research to memo
3. Decision log
4. Project pulse
5. Daily brief / weekly founder review
6. Documentation as we go
